Common mistakes when signing PDFs online
A few small things that make a signed document look less polished, or get it questioned.

Signing too small

A signature shrunk down to fit a tight corner can end up illegible. It's usually better to increase the size slider slightly than to squeeze a signature into an awkward spot.

Wrong page selected

Applying a signature to "Last Page" on a document where the signature block is actually on page one is an easy miss — check where the signature line actually sits before submitting.

Rushed freehand drawing

A signature drawn too quickly with a mouse often comes out as a jagged scribble. Slowing down, or switching to "Type" for a cleaner cursive rendering, both fix this.

Forgetting to check the document first

Skimming the PDF before signing — dates, names, terms — takes a minute and avoids signing something that needed a correction first.

Signing the wrong version of a file

When a document has gone through a few rounds of edits, double-check you're signing the final version rather than an earlier draft still sitting in your downloads folder.

Is a signature made this way legally valid?

For everyday paperwork — internal forms, letters, acknowledgment slips, quotes, informal agreements between two parties — a visually placed signature image like the one this tool creates is widely accepted. Most day-to-day business runs on exactly this kind of signature, and has for years, long before dedicated e-signature platforms existed.

Where it's different is contracts or documents that specifically require a certified digital signature — one that's cryptographically tied to your identity and independently verifiable, rather than just an image placed on the page. Some jurisdictions require that stronger standard for things like property transactions, certain government filings, or high-value legal agreements. If you're not sure which category your document falls into, it's worth checking your country's e-signature rules or asking whoever is requesting the signed document what they require.

Frequently asked questions
Is this tool free?

Yes, completely free. No signup, no watermark, no hidden charges — sign as many PDFs as you need.

Is this a legally binding e-signature?

This tool visually places your signature image onto the PDF. It does not provide cryptographic digital-signature certification required for certain legal contracts — check your country's e-signature rules for formal agreements, and use a certified signing service if that stronger standard is required.

Can I sign multiple pages with different signatures?

This tool places the same signature across your chosen page(s) in one go. To use different signatures per page — for example, initials on inner pages and a full signature on the last — sign the file again for each page with the position set accordingly.

Is my PDF safe?

Yes. Your file is processed securely over SSL and deleted automatically after processing — it isn't stored, shared, or used for anything beyond producing your signed download.

Should I draw or type my signature?

Drawing looks closer to a real pen signature and varies naturally each time, just like signing on paper does. Typing in a signature-style font is faster and gives a consistent result every time, which some people prefer for documents they sign repeatedly.

My trackpad makes the drawn signature look choppy — what can I do?

Trackpads often produce jagged, uneven lines because they sample movement less smoothly than a mouse or touchscreen. If drawing looks rough, switching to "Type" and using the signature-style font usually gives a cleaner result than persisting with a choppy trackpad signature.

Can I sign a PDF from my phone?

Yes — the drawing canvas supports touch input, so you can sign with your finger directly on a phone or tablet screen, and the rest of the tool works the same as on a desktop browser.

Does the signature stay editable after I download the PDF?

No — once placed, the signature becomes part of the flattened page image at that position, the same way a scanned pen signature would. If you need to change it, you'll need to sign the original unsigned PDF again.

What happens to my PDF and signature after I download the file?

Both the uploaded PDF and the signature you drew or typed are deleted from the server shortly after your signed file is generated, so nothing lingers beyond what you download.

A quick habit worth building

If you sign documents more than occasionally, it's worth settling on one signature you're happy with and reusing it, rather than re-drawing a fresh one every time. A consistent signature also makes it easier for the person receiving the document to recognize it as genuinely yours across multiple files, the same way a consistent pen signature does on paper.

Keep the original unsigned PDF somewhere easy to find too — if a document needs to be re-signed later, resigning the clean original is far simpler than trying to remove a signature from an already-signed copy.

None of this takes long once it's set up. The first time you sign a document this way usually takes a minute or two while you settle on a signature you like; every time after that is closer to ten seconds — upload, place, download, done.
