How to Sign a PDF Online for Free (No Printing, No Signup)

Published July 3, 2026 · 4 min read

Print, sign, scan, upload — that used to be the only way to put a signature on a PDF. It doesn't have to be anymore. Here's how to sign any PDF from your browser in under a minute, and what to know before you do.

Why print-sign-scan is disappearing

Signing a document used to mean finding a printer, signing with a pen, scanning it back in, and hoping the file size was small enough to email. Every step in that chain is a place things go wrong — a jammed printer, a scanner that isn't calibrated, a scanned PDF that's suddenly 20MB. Online signing tools skip all of it: your signature is captured digitally and placed directly onto the existing PDF, so the document never leaves your browser as a physical piece of paper.

Two ways to create your signature

Most online signing tools give you two options, and which one you use depends on the situation:

  • Draw it — using your mouse, trackpad, or finger on a touchscreen, you draw your signature freehand. This looks the most like a real signature, with natural variation each time.
  • Type it — you type your name and it's rendered in a cursive, signature-style font. This is faster and gives a perfectly consistent result every time, which some people prefer for repeated use.
Tip: If you're signing from a laptop with a trackpad, typing your signature usually looks cleaner than drawing one — trackpads make freehand drawing choppy. On a phone or tablet, drawing with your finger works well.

Where to place your signature

Once your signature is created, you choose where it goes:

OptionWhen to use it
Bottom-right / Bottom-leftStandard for most letters, forms, and agreements
Top of the pageDocuments that need a signature as an acknowledgment header
All pagesMulti-page contracts that require initials on every page
Just the last pageLong documents where only the final page needs a full signature

Is it legally valid?

For everyday paperwork — internal forms, letters, acknowledgment slips, informal agreements — a visually placed signature image is widely accepted. For contracts or documents that legally require a certified digital signature (with cryptographic verification tied to your identity), check the e-signature rules in your country, since some formal legal documents specifically require that stronger standard rather than a placed signature image.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to print and scan the PDF to sign it?

No. You can draw your signature directly with your mouse or finger, or type your name in a signature-style font, and place it straight onto the PDF — no printer or scanner needed.

Is an online signature on a PDF legally valid?

A visually placed signature image is accepted for many everyday documents like forms, letters, and internal approvals. For contracts requiring certified digital signatures with cryptographic verification, check your country's e-signature laws, as some formal legal documents require a certified digital signature service.

Can I sign every page of a multi-page PDF at once?

Yes. Most online signing tools let you choose to apply your signature to just the first page, just the last page, or every page in the document in a single pass.

Will my signature look the same every time?

If you draw your signature, it will vary slightly each time just like a pen signature does. If you type your name using a signature-style font, it will look identical every time you use it.
