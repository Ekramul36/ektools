Need a digital signature for a document, email, or contract? EkZapp's Signature Generator lets you create one in seconds — completely free, with no signup required. Draw your signature naturally using your mouse or touchscreen, or type your name and choose from elegant cursive fonts to generate a signature style instantly. Download it as a transparent PNG, ready to insert into PDFs, Word documents, or online forms without a white box showing around it.

This tool is especially useful for freelancers, students, and professionals who frequently need to sign digital documents without printing a page, signing it by hand, scanning it back in, and cropping the image — a process that can take longer than the rest of the paperwork combined. With a saved signature PNG on your device, signing a PDF becomes a matter of pasting an image in the right spot, something that takes seconds once the signature itself exists.

Everything happens in your browser using HTML canvas — your signature is generated instantly and isn't stored on any server. That matters for a signature specifically, since it's a piece of personal identity you'd rather not have sitting in a database somewhere. Close the tab and nothing about the session remains; the only copy that exists afterward is the PNG file you chose to download to your own device.

The three typed-signature fonts were picked to cover different tones: Dancing Script leans casual and friendly, Great Vibes reads more formal and flowing, and Sacramento sits somewhere in between with a slightly more compact, modern cursive. If you draw your signature instead, four pen colors — black, blue, red, and green — are available, so you can match a convention some documents expect, like blue ink for an "original" signature versus black for a photocopy-safe one.

Tips

Getting a signature that looks natural

1

Sign slower than you think you should

Drawing with a mouse or trackpad is less precise than pen on paper — slowing down slightly keeps the strokes smoother and more legible.

2

Use a touchscreen if you have one

Signing with a finger on a phone or tablet usually produces a more natural-looking result than a mouse on a desktop.

3

Try more than one typed font

Dancing Script, Great Vibes, and Sacramento each carry a different tone — preview your name in all three before picking one.

4

Keep a saved copy for reuse

Once you're happy with a signature PNG, save it somewhere easy to find on your device so you're not regenerating it for every document.

Good to know

Where a digital signature works — and where it doesn't

✓

Informal documents and forms

Consent slips, internal office forms, personal declarations, and similar low-stakes paperwork are a good fit for this kind of visual signature.

✓

Email and personal branding

A signature image in an email footer or a personal document adds a handwritten touch without any legal requirement attached.

!

Legally binding contracts

For contracts, deeds, or agreements that need a legally recognized e-signature, check the e-signature laws in your jurisdiction and consider a certified e-signature service instead.

✓

Design mockups and templates

Designers and marketers often need a placeholder or sample signature for a mockup, certificate design, or template — this tool covers that quickly.

!

Financial and government documents

Bank forms, notarized documents, and official government paperwork typically require an in-person or officially verified signature — check the specific requirement before relying on any visual signature image.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is this signature legally binding?
This tool creates a visual signature image. For legally binding e-signatures on contracts, check your local e-signature laws — this tool is best for informal documents, forms, and personal use.
Does my signature get uploaded anywhere?
No. Everything happens directly in your browser — nothing is sent to our servers, so your signature stays completely private.
What format is the download?
A transparent PNG image, so you can paste it directly onto documents, PDFs, or images without a white background showing behind it.
Can I use this on my phone?
Yes, the draw pad works with touch input on mobile and tablets, and the typed-signature mode works identically on any screen size.
Do I need to install any software?
No, everything works directly in your browser — no downloads, extensions, or app installs needed.
Can I change the color of a drawn signature?
Yes, choose from four pen colors — black, blue, red, or green — before or while drawing.
How do I get a signature with no background at all?
Both the drawn and typed signature download as a transparent PNG by default, so there's no white box around your signature when pasted onto a document.
Can I redo my signature if I make a mistake while drawing?
Yes, tap Clear to wipe the canvas and draw again as many times as you need before downloading.
Which is better — drawn or typed signature?
A drawn signature looks more like a handwritten one if you're comfortable using a mouse or touchscreen; a typed signature in a cursive font is quicker and more consistent every time.
Can I use the same signature on multiple documents?
Yes, once downloaded as a PNG, you can reuse that same image file across as many documents as you like.
What's the difference between this and an official e-signature service?
Official e-signature services add identity verification, timestamps, and audit trails required for legal enforceability on contracts. This tool creates the visual signature image itself, without those verification layers.
Can I make my signature bigger or smaller after downloading?
The PNG downloads at a fixed size, but most PDF editors and document tools let you resize an inserted image freely once it's placed on the page.
Does the tool work offline?
The page itself needs to load once from the internet, but the drawing and typing both run locally in your browser without needing an active connection after that.
Can I create more than one signature style and pick between them?
Yes, there's no limit to how many times you can draw or type a new version — download as many variations as you'd like and keep the one you prefer.
Is a typed cursive signature considered less genuine than a drawn one?
For informal use, both are equally valid as a visual mark — a typed cursive signature simply trades some handwritten variation for speed and consistency.
About this tool

Why it's free, and how it stays that way

The Signature Generator is one of more than fifty free tools on EkZapp, alongside PDF editing, resume building, and invoice generation. Because the drawing and typing both happen entirely in your browser using canvas, the tool costs very little to run even as more people use it — there's no server-side image processing to pay for. That's also the privacy story: nothing about your signature needs to touch a server in the first place, so keeping it private and keeping it free turned out to be the same decision.

A signature is a small but personal thing — it's meant to represent your identity, so a tool built around it should be straightforward about what happens to your data. Here, the answer is simple: nothing is stored, nothing is logged tied to your signature, and the only copy that exists afterward is the file you chose to save on your own device.

Keeping the tool this narrow in scope is also what makes it fast. There's no account system to check, no history to load, and no dashboard to render before you can start drawing or typing — the page opens and the canvas is ready. That simplicity is a deliberate trade-off: a tool that does one thing without extra features around it tends to load faster and behave more predictably than a bundled document suite trying to do everything at once.
