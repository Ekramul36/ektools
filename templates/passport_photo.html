📖 How to Use — Step by Step Guide

▼
  • 1
    Upload Your Photo Click the upload area or drag & drop your photo. Use a clear, front-facing photo with good lighting. Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP (max 10MB).
  • 2
    Select Photo Size Choose the country/visa type from the dropdown. India passport needs 35×45 mm, US passport needs 2×2 inch (51×51 mm), and so on.
  • 3
    Choose Number of Copies Select how many photos you want on the A4 sheet — 4, 8, 12, or 16. More copies = better value when printing at a photo studio.
  • 4
    Pick Background Color Most countries require a plain white background. Some (like USA) allow off-white. Click a preset swatch or use the color picker for custom colors.
  • 5
    Generate & Preview Click "Generate & Preview" — your A4 photo sheet will appear on screen. Check if it looks good before downloading.
  • 6
    Download & Print Click "Download JPEG" to save the file. Then print it at any photo studio or print shop at actual size (A4, 300 DPI) — do NOT resize while printing!

💡 Tips for a Perfect Passport Photo

  • Use a plain background while taking the photo — white wall or light-coloured wall works best
  • Face the camera directly — no tilting, no sunglasses, no hat
  • Make sure your full face is visible with neutral expression (slight smile is usually okay)
  • Use natural lighting — avoid harsh shadows on the face
  • Photo should be taken recently (within last 6 months for most countries)
  • When printing, tell the studio to print at actual size / 100% scale on A4 glossy photo paper
Frequently asked questions
Is this tool free?
Yes, completely free! No signup, no watermark, no hidden charges.
Will my photo be saved on the server?
Your photo is processed temporarily and deleted automatically. We do not store or share your photos.
What if my background is not white?
This tool does not automatically remove backgrounds. For best results, take a photo in front of a plain wall. Or use our Remove Background tool first, then come back here.
Can I use this for Indian passport / Aadhaar / visa?
Yes! Select "India — 35×45 mm" for Indian passport, Aadhaar card, PAN card, and most Indian government documents. For US visa select the USA 51×51 mm option.
How do I print correctly?
Open the downloaded JPEG and print at A4 size with "Actual Size" or "100%" scale selected. Use glossy photo paper at a photo studio for best results.
Why does the head size matter, not just the photo dimensions?
Most passport rules specify how much of the frame your face should fill, not just the outer paper size — a correctly sized photo where the face is too small or too large relative to the frame can still be rejected, so keep your face centered and reasonably close-up before uploading.
