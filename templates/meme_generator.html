EkZapp's Meme Generator lets you upload any picture and turn it into a shareable meme without downloading an app, installing a plugin, or creating an account. Most free meme tools online lock you into a rigid top-text, bottom-text template that was designed for the classic image-macro format of the early internet — fine for a handful of old formats, but limiting the moment you want a caption in the middle of the frame, a reaction bubble near a face, or two lines of text stacked in a corner. EkZapp takes a different approach: you add as many independent text boxes as you like, and each one can be dragged to exactly the spot on the image where it belongs. There's no fixed layout to fight against.

Every text box carries its own settings, so you're not stuck with one look across the whole meme. Pick from seven fonts built for the format — Impact and Anton for the bold, classic image-macro look; Bangers and Permanent Marker for a hand-drawn, comic-style caption; Bebas Neue for tall, condensed headline text; Oswald for a cleaner, modern feel; or plain Inter if you just want readable text. Each box also gets its own fill color and outline color, controlled with a simple color picker, so you can match a caption to the mood of the photo or keep it high-contrast for readability. Stack multiple boxes, resize them independently with a slider, and delete or edit any of them without disturbing the rest.

Beyond text, the editor includes twelve draggable stickers — fire, skull, sunglasses, heart, laughing and crying-laughing faces, a thumbs up, eyes, a speech bubble and more — each one independently resizable and positionable, the same way text boxes work. Five one-tap filters (grayscale, sepia, bright, dark and invert) are applied to the base image instantly, so you can set the tone of the photo before adding captions. When the meme is ready, a single tap downloads it as a JPG with zero watermark — what you see on the canvas is exactly what you get in the file.

Privacy is built into how the tool works, not bolted on afterward: your image is never uploaded to any server. The whole editor runs on HTML5 canvas directly inside your browser tab — uploading, editing, filtering and exporting all happen on your own device, and closing the tab clears everything. That also means it works the same way on a budget Android phone on a slow mobile connection as it does on a laptop, since there's no upload wait and no server round-trip for every change. The canvas is touch-optimized, so dragging text boxes and stickers with a finger feels as natural as using a mouse.

Tips

Getting a caption that actually lands

1

Keep the outline thick on busy photos

Increase the outline width when the background behind your text has a lot of detail or color variation — it keeps the caption readable at a glance.

2

Leave breathing room around each box

Avoid placing text right at the very edge of the photo — a small margin keeps the caption from looking cropped on different screen sizes.

3

Match the font to the tone

Impact and Anton read as classic and punchy; Permanent Marker and Bangers feel more playful and hand-drawn. Pick the one that fits the joke.

4

Don't overcrowd with stickers

One or two well-placed stickers usually reads better than five — they should support the caption, not compete with it.

5

Preview before you download

Zoom in on the canvas or check it on your phone screen before exporting — text that looks fine on a laptop can shrink awkwardly on mobile.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is my image uploaded to a server?
No. Everything is processed directly in your browser using HTML canvas — your image never leaves your device, and nothing is stored anywhere once you close the tab.
Does the meme have a watermark?
No watermark is added, and there's no small logo tucked into a corner either. Your downloaded meme is clean and ready to share exactly as you designed it.
Can I move the text anywhere on the image?
Yes. Add as many text boxes as you like using "Add Text," then drag each one individually to any position on the image — there's no fixed top/bottom layout to work around.
Can I change the font and color?
Yes, each text box has its own font family (choose from 7 options), size slider, fill color and outline color, so different captions on the same meme can each look different if you want.
Is it free?
Yes, 100% free with unlimited use, including all filters, stickers and fonts. There's no daily limit, no watermark to pay to remove, and no account needed.
Does it work on mobile phones?
Yes. The editor is touch-friendly, so you can upload a photo, add stickers, and drag text boxes and stickers with your finger on any Android or iOS phone or tablet.
What image formats can I upload?
You can upload any common image format your device supports for photos — JPG, PNG, WEBP and similar. The finished meme downloads as a JPG file.
Is there a limit to how many text boxes or stickers I can add?
No hard limit is built in. You can keep adding text boxes and stickers, though for a clean, readable meme it usually helps to keep the layout simple.
Can I undo a mistake while editing?
You can delete any individual text box or sticker with the ✕ button on its card and re-add it, or click "New Image" to start completely fresh with a different photo.
Do I need to install anything to use this?
No installation is needed. The Meme Generator runs entirely in your web browser, so it works the same way on Chrome, Safari, Firefox or Edge without any extension or app.
What resolution does the downloaded meme have?
The output matches the size the image is displayed on the canvas, capped for smooth in-browser editing, which is sharp enough for WhatsApp, Instagram, X and most social platforms.
