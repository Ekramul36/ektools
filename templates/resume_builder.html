Avoid

Common resume mistakes

1

Using a personal email that looks unprofessional

An email like "coolguy99@..." undercuts an otherwise strong resume — a simple name-based address reads better to recruiters.

2

Writing a generic summary

"Hardworking individual seeking opportunities" says nothing specific — a summary should mention the actual field and one concrete strength.

3

Listing duties instead of impact

"Handled customer queries" is weaker than "Resolved an average of 40 customer queries a day with a 95% satisfaction rate."

4

Leaving the skills section too vague

"Good communication skills" is expected of everyone — pair soft skills with specific tools, languages, or technical skills relevant to the role.

Tips

What makes a resume worth reading

A

Lead with results, not duties

Instead of "responsible for sales," write what changed because of the work — a number, a percentage, or a concrete outcome reads stronger.

B

Keep the summary to 2-3 lines

A short summary gets read in full; a long one gets skimmed or skipped entirely by a recruiter scanning dozens of resumes.

C

Match skills to the job posting

Update the skills field for each application so the most relevant keywords for that specific role appear clearly.

D

Order experience newest first

Recruiters scan top to bottom — your most recent and most relevant role should always come first in the list.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is EkZapp's Resume Builder really free?
Yes, completely free. There are no premium templates, hidden fees, or watermarks on your downloaded resume, and there's no limit on how many times you can generate one.
Do I need to create an account to build a resume?
No signup is required. You can build and download your resume without providing an email address or creating a password.
Is the resume template ATS-friendly?
Yes, the layout uses clean, standard formatting with clear section headings that Applicant Tracking Systems used by most companies can easily parse and read.
Can I add more than one work experience?
Yes, click "Add Another Experience" to add as many entries as you need, and the same works for education entries — there's no fixed limit.
What file format do I get?
Your resume downloads as a PDF, which is the most widely accepted format for job applications and displays consistently on every device.
Is this suitable for a fresher with no work experience?
Yes. You can leave the work experience section blank or use it for internships and projects, and lead with your education and skills instead.
Can I edit my resume after downloading it?
The PDF itself isn't editable, but you can return to the form, update any field, and generate a fresh PDF in seconds whenever your details change.
Is my personal information stored anywhere?
Your details are used only to generate the PDF for that session and are not kept in a permanent database tied to your identity.
Does the resume support Indian phone number and address formats?
Yes, the phone and location fields accept any format you type, including Indian numbers with a country code and city, state formatting.
Can I use this on my mobile phone?
Yes, the form and download work the same way on a phone browser as they do on a desktop.
How long should my resume be?
For most freshers and early-career applicants, one page is enough. Experienced professionals with many roles can extend to a second page if needed.
Can I list skills I'm still learning?
Yes, but be honest about your proficiency — you can mention it's a developing skill in the summary if you're asked about it in an interview.
Should I include a photo on my resume?
For most private-sector applications in India, a photo isn't necessary and the form doesn't include one — some government forms may ask for it separately alongside the resume.
Can I generate resumes for different job applications separately?
Yes, since there's no account or saved state, you can fill the form fresh for each application and tailor the summary and skills each time.

About this tool

Why it's free, and how it stays that way

The Resume Builder is one of more than fifty free tools on EkZapp, a small, independently run collection of everyday utilities that also includes PDF editing, image compression, and other document tools. It stays free because generating a PDF from a form is a lightweight server operation, which keeps costs manageable without needing to charge for downloads or gate templates behind a paywall. The layout is intentionally simple rather than heavily designed, because a resume's job is to be read correctly by both people and software — not to look impressive at the cost of being scanned incorrectly.

There's also a practical reason the form stays this straightforward: job searching is stressful enough without a tool adding friction on top of it. A resume builder that asks for a credit card before letting you download the one document you actually need for an application defeats its own purpose. The goal here is the opposite — get you from a blank form to a finished, usable PDF as quickly as possible, so the time and energy you have goes toward the actual job search rather than fighting with formatting or signup screens.
