How to Make a Meme Online for Free — Fonts, Stickers & Filters

Published July 6, 2026 · 4 min read

Memes are everywhere — group chats, social media, work Slack channels. But most free meme makers either lock the good features behind a paywall, slap a watermark on your download, or force you to sign up before you can save anything. EkZapp's Meme Generator does none of that, and it now comes with a full set of customization tools that go well beyond the classic "top text, bottom text" format.

Here's how to use every feature to make a meme that actually looks the way you want it to.

1. Upload your image

Head to the Meme Generator, click the upload area, and choose any photo from your device. There's no size limit hassle and no signup screen in the way — your image loads straight into the editor and stays entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server, so it's private by default.

2. Add as many text boxes as you need

Unlike basic meme tools that only give you a fixed top and bottom caption, EkZapp lets you click "Add Text" to drop in a new text box anywhere. Add one, two, or five — there's no limit. Each text box can be dragged directly on the image with your mouse or finger, so you're not stuck with the classic layout if your idea needs the caption somewhere else.

3. Pick a font that matches the vibe

Every text box has its own font selector, with classic meme fonts like Impact and Anton, plus more playful options like Bangers and Permanent Marker for a hand-drawn look. You can also adjust the font size with a slider, so a short punchline can go big and bold while a longer caption stays readable.

4. Customize text and outline color

Click the color swatches next to any text box to change both the fill color and the outline color independently. White text with a black outline is the classic look, but if your background is light, flipping to dark text with a white outline will read much better.

5. Drop in stickers

Click the "Stickers" button to open a palette of emoji-style stickers — fire, laughing faces, thumbs up, sunglasses, and more. Tap one to add it to your meme, then drag it into place and resize it with the slider. It's a quick way to add extra punch without needing any design skill.

6. Apply a filter

If the photo itself needs a mood shift, the Filters row lets you switch between Grayscale, Sepia, Bright, Dark, and Invert with one click. This applies to the image only, so your text and stickers stay sharp and readable on top.

7. Download — no watermark, no signup

Once it looks right, hit Download Meme. You'll get a clean JPG file with everything baked in, ready to post or send. No EkZapp logo, no signup wall, no limit on how many memes you make.
