How to Compress PDF to 100KB or 1MB Online for Free

Published July 3, 2026 · 4 min read

If you've ever tried uploading a PDF to an SSC, Railway, or Bank PO exam form and hit an error like "File size must be under 100KB," you're not alone. Here's exactly how to shrink your PDF to the required size in under a minute — free, no signup.

Why exam forms demand such tiny PDF sizes

Most government exam portals in India were built years ago on servers with strict upload limits, often 100KB to 1MB per file. During peak form-submission periods with lakhs of applicants uploading documents simultaneously, these limits keep the servers from crashing. The result: your scanned certificate, mark sheet, or signed document — which might easily be 2-5MB straight out of a phone camera — needs to be squeezed down dramatically before the form will accept it.

Tips to get the smallest file size without losing quality

  • Scan documents in black-and-white or grayscale mode instead of color when the content allows it.
  • Avoid scanning at very high DPI (300 DPI is usually more than enough for text documents).
  • If your PDF is mostly one scanned image, consider converting it to JPG first and using EkZapp's Image Size Reducer to hit an exact KB target, then convert back to PDF.
  • Remove any blank or duplicate pages before compressing.
