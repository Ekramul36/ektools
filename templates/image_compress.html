Why Compress an Image Online?

Photos taken on modern phone cameras are often several megabytes each, which makes them slow to upload, awkward to email, and sometimes too large for websites or online forms that enforce a size limit. EkZapp's Image Compressor reduces JPG, PNG, and WebP file sizes significantly while keeping the picture looking sharp, all directly in your browser with no software to install.

A live before-and-after size comparison lets you see exactly how much space you saved, and the adjustable quality slider gives you full control over the balance between file size and visual clarity.

Common Reasons to Compress an Image

  • Website performance: Smaller images load faster, which improves page speed and user experience.
  • Form & portal uploads: Many job, visa, and government portals cap image size — compression brings large photos within limit.
  • Email attachments: Avoid slow uploads or bounced emails caused by oversized photos.
  • Storage saving: Free up space on your phone or cloud drive by shrinking photo libraries.
  • Social media & messaging: Faster sharing over WhatsApp, Instagram, or other apps with smaller file sizes.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much will my image be compressed?
Typically 50-80% smaller depending on the original image and quality setting you choose.
Which formats are supported?
JPG, PNG, and WebP formats are supported for compression.
Is this tool free?
Yes, completely free. No signup required.
Will compressing reduce the visual quality of my photo?
The quality slider lets you control the tradeoff. Around 80% quality, most images look nearly identical to the original while being significantly smaller.
Why do I need to compress an image before uploading it?
Many websites, forms, and portals set a maximum upload size, and large photos from phone cameras often exceed it. Compressing brings the file within limit.
Is my uploaded image stored anywhere?
No. The image is processed and returned to you, then automatically removed — it is not stored or shared.
