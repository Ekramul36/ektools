What is a HEIC file?
The format behind every photo your iPhone takes, and why it doesn't always travel well.

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is the photo format Apple made the default on iPhones starting with iOS 11 in 2017. It's built on the same underlying compression technology as HEIF video, and it does a genuinely better job than JPG at squeezing a smaller file out of the same image quality — often close to half the file size for a similar-looking photo. That's a real win for storage space and iCloud backups.

The catch is compatibility. HEIC support has to be built into whatever app or device is opening the file, and a lot of software still doesn't include it — older Android phones, many Windows photo viewers, some web upload forms, and plenty of older editing tools. JPG, by contrast, has been a universal standard since the 1990s and opens without question basically anywhere. Converting HEIC to JPG trades a bit of that storage efficiency for near-universal compatibility.

Windows 10 and 11 can technically view HEIC files after installing an extension from the Microsoft Store, and recent Android versions have gradually added partial support — but "technically supported" and "opens instantly without a second thought" are different things. A JPG never requires that extra step for the person receiving it, which is the whole reason the format mismatch still causes friction nearly a decade after HEIC was introduced.

Common issues and how to avoid them
A few things worth knowing before converting a batch of photos.

"Unsupported file type" on upload

This is the exact problem HEIC to JPG conversion solves — convert first, then upload the resulting JPG to whatever form or platform rejected the original.

Batch of photos looks huge in total

JPGs run somewhat larger than the source HEIC files. If total size matters for email or storage, lower the quality slider before converting — 75-80% still looks sharp for most everyday photos.

Live Photos only convert the still frame

An iPhone Live Photo is technically a HEIC image plus a short video clip. This tool converts the still image; the motion portion isn't part of a HEIC to JPG conversion by definition.

Accidentally selecting the wrong files

The file list shows every selected photo with its size before converting, so it's easy to spot and remove one that doesn't belong before hitting convert.

What's the difference between HEIC and HEIF?

HEIF is the broader container format; HEIC is the specific image variant of it that iPhones use for photos. In practice the two terms are used almost interchangeably for still images, and this tool accepts both extensions.
